# Annotated References

This file captures why specific reference screenshots matter.
Use it to train consistency of taste, not to copy layouts literally.

## Crextio.png

What is working:
- soft premium look
- rounded cards
- warm highlight and glow treatment
- calm, light dashboard atmosphere
- blurry background and gentle background gradient

Do not copy literally:
- too decorative for serious accounting by default
- too much photo and illustration emphasis
- top tabs are not desired; prefer a side panel

Reusable rules to extract:
- use soft, premium card treatment with rounded corners
- keep spacing rhythmic and calm instead of dense and noisy
- create hierarchy with softness and composition, not aggressive contrast
- when using glow or gradients, keep them atmospheric and subtle

Style tags:
- airy
- premium
- rounded
- warm
- dashboard

## Salesforce.webp

What is working:
- list design feels strong and reusable
- buttons feel clean
- simple, minimal, modern presentation
- useful typography contrast between bold, normal, and italic text

Do not copy literally:
- top tabs are not desired; prefer a side panel

Reusable rules to extract:
- use this as inspiration for list rows and list-detail interfaces
- keep action buttons compact, clean, and visually controlled
- use typography contrast to create hierarchy inside dense UI
- preserve premium rounded card treatment even in utility-heavy screens

Style tags:
- list
- premium
- rounded

## Current Taste Synthesis

Strong signals so far:
- you prefer premium rounded surfaces
- you like calm hierarchy more than loud decoration
- you like soft gradients and atmospheric backgrounds when they stay restrained
- you prefer side panels over top-tab navigation
- you want list screens to feel clean, modern, and highly reusable
- you want a design system that works for serious business apps, not just showcase dashboards

Early system direction:
- primary app style: clean premium B2B with soft rounded surfaces
- secondary pattern source: modern list/detail layouts with strong typographic hierarchy
- avoid over-decorative hero treatments unless the product specifically benefits from them
